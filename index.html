Game of Life

The Game of Life is a classic cellular automaton created by mathematician John Conway in 1970. It is a zero-player game, which means that it does not require any input from a player once it starts. The game takes place on a two-dimensional grid of cells, where each cell can be either alive or dead.

The rules of the game are simple: a live cell with two or three live neighbors survives to the next generation, while a live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies of loneliness and a live cell with more than three live neighbors dies of overcrowding. A dead cell with exactly three live neighbors becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.

Despite its simplicity, the Game of Life is capable of producing a wide variety of complex patterns, including gliders, spaceships, and oscillators. It has become a popular subject of study in computer science, mathematics, and physics, and has even been used to model real-world phenomena such as the spread of diseases and the behavior of traffic.

I developed this game in C for a school project, and it is important to me because it was my first real computer science project.

Tic Tac Toe Artificial Intelligence

I developed a Tic Tac Toe game using Python and TensorFlow for a school project. The game uses a neural network model trained with TensorFlow to predict the optimal moves for the computer player. The model is based on a dataset of thousands of games played by human players, and it is trained to analyze the board state and predict the best move to make based on the current game situation.

The game is designed to be highly interactive, with a user-friendly interface and intuitive controls. The neural network model makes the game challenging and dynamic, as the computer player adapts its strategies based on the player's moves. The game also includes features such as a leaderboard to track high scores and a replay mode to review past games.

Overall, this project was a great opportunity for me to learn about machine learning and neural networks in a practical and engaging way. I am proud of the results and look forward to continuing to develop my skills in this exciting field.
